Chicken Noodle Soup

Description

Chicken noodle soup originated in Southeast Asia, with people making it for at least 7,000 years. Even the Ancient Greeks had their own version of chicken broth that was believed to have healing properties.

Whether you're trying to kick a seasonal cold or just craving comfort food on a chilly day, chicken noodle soup may be the first meal you think of.

Our Chicken Noodle Soup recipe will not only take you to the streets of China, but also make you healthier. All the while being tasty. Hard to believe, right? Just try our recipe and give us your feedback!

Ingredients

  • 8 cups of water
  • 2 skinless, boneless chicken breast
  • 2 skinless chicken thighs
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 4-5 whole peppercorns
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 onion
  • 1 stark celery
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 125g egg noodles

Steps

  1. To the soup pot, add 2 chicken breast, 2 chicken thighs, 2 bay leaves, the peppercorn and a pinch of salt.
  2. Pour water and boil on medium heat, then lower the heat and cook for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken and cool it. Shred to bite-sized pieces. Remove the leaves and corns.
  4. Melt the butter and saute the onions, celery and carrots for 5 minutes, then add 1 garlic clove and cook for 30 seconds.
  5. Pour the broth on vegetables then add the chicken in the pot. Then add the noodles and cook for 8-10 minutes.
  6. Sprinkle with fresh dill and parsley before serving. We hope you'll like it!
